Internalizing the Benefits of Visual Prompting

Aiden Blair

Introduction

In the past, we have seen demonstrations of vision models learning to become robust to adversarial perturbations to their inputs, which greatly improved the apparent interpretability of their activation maps. In this work, I examine methods to do something similar to improve the interpretability of activations using beneficial perturbations (called Visual Prompts in other literature) instead:

  • optimizing over perturbations and model weights to minimize the size of the perturbation, so as to make the perturbation unnecessary
  • performing knowledge distillation between the base model and the model augmented with the perturbation
